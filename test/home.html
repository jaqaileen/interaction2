Flowers of
New York City

In the concrete jungle of New York City,
flowers serve as colorful beacons of nature,
softening the urban landscape and providing
moments of respite and beauty amidst the
hustle and bustle of city life.

With a diverse array of flora
gracing its parks, sidewalks, and
green spaces, flowers play a
vital role in maintaining the city's
ecological balance and
enhancing its aesthetic appeal.

New York City's spontaneous vascular
plant flora boasts an impressive 2,029
species spread across 160 families
and 726 genera, contributing to the rich
tapestry of biodiversity within the
urban environment.
